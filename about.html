Our Story

How Medini Welfare Society started

Founded in 2019 by visionary Chero community leaders and educators, Medini Welfare Society serves as a vital platform where change-makers gather to drive grassroots social progress and community upliftment. The organization was born from a shared realization across rural villages: bright, ambitious children were losing access to opportunities simply due to a lack of academic resources. What began as a single, humble after-school classroom for twenty students has evolved into a dynamic collective network featuring 12 active learning centers, specialized scholarship programs for girls, vocational courses for young adults, and vital healthcare camps. Driven by the ultimate guiding principle of "Nation First," the society bridges historical pride with future-focused leadership. Every gathering and initiative organized by the NGO focuses heavily on modern education, cultural preservation, and building robust socio-political awareness. By honoring and keeping the resilient legacy of the Chero heritage alive, Medini Welfare Society inspires the upcoming generation to reclaim their narrative. Today, working hand-in-hand with dedicated local volunteers and partners, the society is actively cultivating an ecosystem where rural youth are equipped with the skills, confidence, and leadership necessary to seize every opportunity available in India across all spheres of life.

Mission & Vision

What we're working toward

Mission — To widen access to education, health and livelihood opportunities for underserved communities through direct, accountable, community-rooted programs.

Vision — A future where a child's postcode no longer determines their access to a good education or a fair start in life.

Values — Transparency with donors, dignity for the people we serve, and decisions made with communities rather than for them.
